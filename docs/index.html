Before You Renew

Start 4-6 months ahead

Use RateCompass to compare your current rate, renewal timing, and fixed or variable exposure before accepting a renewal offer.

1Ask your lender for the renewal offer and payout details.
2Compare at least one broker or competing lender quote.
3Use My Impact to test payment changes before choosing a term.
↓

When Rates Fall

Look for opportunity

Lower rates can help borrowers, but they can also reduce savings and GIC returns.

1Review variable mortgage, HELOC, and line of credit costs.
2Watch savings promos before moving cash.
3Check whether locking a GIC still fits your timeline.
↑

When Rates Rise

Protect cash flow

Higher rates can raise borrowing costs but may improve some savings and GIC opportunities.

1Stress-test mortgage and debt payments.
2Prioritize expensive prime-linked debt.
3Compare GIC terms before chasing the highest posted rate.
?

Questions To Ask

Use before a call

Bring clear questions to your bank, broker, or advisor so rate conversations stay practical.

1What rate, term, and payment options are available today?
2What penalties, fees, or restrictions apply?
3How would this choice change if rates moved by 0.50%?
