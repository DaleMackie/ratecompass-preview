When Rates Fall

Look for opportunity

Lower rates can help borrowers, but they can also reduce savings and GIC returns.

1Review variable mortgage, HELOC, and line of credit costs.
2Watch savings promos before moving cash.
3Check whether locking a GIC still fits your timeline.
↑

When Rates Rise

Protect cash flow

Higher rates can raise borrowing costs but may improve some savings and GIC opportunities.

1Stress-test mortgage and debt payments.
2Prioritize expensive prime-linked debt.
3Compare GIC terms before chasing the highest posted rate.

Terms Index

Plain-language rate terminology

Mortgages And Debt

Default insured mortgage

A mortgage where insurance protects the lender if the borrower cannot repay. These rates can differ from uninsured mortgages because lender risk is different.

Uninsured mortgage

A mortgage that does not have default insurance. Rates may be higher when the loan, property, or borrower profile creates more lender risk.

Variable mortgage

A mortgage where the rate is tied to prime. The cost can change when lenders change prime after Bank of Canada decisions.

Prime rate

A reference lending rate set by banks, often influenced by Bank of Canada policy-rate changes. Variable mortgages, HELOCs, and lines of credit are commonly priced from prime.

Prime + spread

A rate shown as prime plus an extra amount, such as Prime +0.50%. HELOCs and lines of credit are often quoted this way.

HELOC

A home equity line of credit secured by your property. It is usually quoted as a spread over prime.

GICs

Non-redeemable GIC

A GIC where the money is usually locked in until maturity. The tradeoff is often a stronger posted rate.

Redeemable GIC

A GIC that may allow early access before maturity. The tradeoff is usually a lower rate than a non-redeemable GIC.

Cashable GIC

A GIC designed for easier access to funds, often after a short waiting period. The rate is usually lower because flexibility has value.

Savings And Comparisons

High-interest savings account

A savings account that aims to pay more than a standard account. The rate can depend on the institution, balance, and conditions.

Promo rate

A temporary savings or GIC offer. It can be attractive, but expiry dates, limits, and conditions matter.

Weighted benchmark

A sample comparison point that gives more influence to major Canadian bank rates while still considering smaller or online institution samples.

Quote rating

A plain-language score that compares a quoted rate or prime spread against the current sample benchmark.
